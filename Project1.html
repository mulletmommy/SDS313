Variable Names of Dataset

  • Title: Title of Book
  • Author: Author of Book
  • AuthorBirthYear: Year that Author was born
  • AuthorDeathYear: Year that Author died
  • PublicationYear: Year that the book was published
  • GradeLevel: The Automated Readibilty Index(ARI) of a book. It calculates a number that indicates how hard or easy it is to read a book. The higher the score, the more difficult it is to read. Each grade is assigned a range of ARI numbers that represent the difficulty.
  • AvgLettersPerWord: The average amount of letters per Word
  • AvgSentenceLength: The average number of words within each sentence
  • NumberSentences: Number of sentences in the book
  • Downloads: Number of downloads for that specific book

My chosen outcome for this lab will be the GradeLevel variable. My three predictors that I will be investigating in this lab are AuthorDeathYear, AvgLettersPerWord, and AvgSentenceLength.

GradeLevel vs.AuthorDeathYear, AvgLettersPerWord, and AvgSentenceLength

The correlation coefficient between ARI Score and Year of Authors Death is -0.19, indicating that there is likely no relationship between these variables.

The correlation coefficient between ARI Score and Average Letters Per Word is 0.27, indicating that there is a weak, positive correlation. Since the strength of this relationship is minimal, it may not have substantial practical significance in our context.

The correlation coefficient between ARI Score and Average Letters Per Word is 0.95, indicating that there is a strong, positive correlation. Since the strength is quite strong, the relationship has high practical significance in our context.

Conclusion

There is close to no relationship between ARI Score and Year of Authors Death, some weak, positive relationship between ARI Score and Average Letters Per Word, and a strong, positive relationship between ARI Score and Average Sentence Length. I find it very interesting that there is a weak relationship between ARI Score and Average Letters Per Word, as the formula for ARI uses Average Letters Per Word to calculate the score. The data on Project Gutenburg mightve used a different variable to calculate ARI, or mightve had some erros. ARI also uses the length of sentence to calculate the end Score, which we can see through the strong relationship between the two. I thought maybe there mightve been relationship between how old a text is and how difficult it is to read, but it makes sense that there isnt any relationship, as older texts have been translated to fit the modern public.
